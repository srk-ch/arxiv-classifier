About the Project

üéØ What is arXiv Paper Classifier?

arXiv Paper Classifier is an advanced machine learning application designed to automatically categorize research papers into their appropriate academic domains. Using state-of-the-art natural language processing and machine learning techniques, our system can accurately classify papers across multiple scientific disciplines.

The system is trained on thousands of research papers from the arXiv repository and achieves an impressive 87.56% accuracy rate. Whether you're a researcher, student, or academic institution, our classifier helps you organize and discover relevant research efficiently.

ü§ñ How It Works

Our classifier uses a hybrid approach combining multiple techniques:

1. Text Preprocessing: We clean and normalize your paper's title and abstract, removing mathematical notation and formatting that could interfere with classification.

2. Feature Extraction: We use TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) to convert text into numerical features, combined with domain-specific keyword matching to capture field-specific terminology.

3. CatBoost Classification: Our CatBoost machine learning model analyzes these features to predict the most appropriate category with confidence scores.
